# Phase-Specific Charge Questions

Adapted from DOE G 413.3-9A and DOE/SC Independent Project Review Process.
Apply the charge question set for the **phase** and **work product type** combination.

For private-sector gates, use the mapping table below to select the closest equivalent.

## CD-0 / Conceptual: Approve Mission Need

### Risk Register

At this phase, the register is expected to be high-level — identifying program-level
risks, not project-level risks. Expect 5–10 risks. Lack of granularity is acceptable;
absence of any risk documentation is not.

**Charge Questions:**
1. Has a credible performance gap been identified and documented, with risks to filling that gap acknowledged?
2. Are the major risks and constraints associated with the mission need identified at a high level sufficient for this stage?
3. Is the cost range reasonable and appropriately wide given the limited scope definition?

### Schedule

At CD-0, a full IMS is not expected. A milestone schedule showing major phases and key
decision points is appropriate.

**Charge Questions:**
1. Does a high-level milestone schedule exist that identifies the path from approval of mission need to CD-1?
2. Are the phase durations reasonable given the scope complexity and expected level of
   studies, analysis of alternatives, and early design effort?
3. Are any early long-lead items (site selection, environmental studies, technology assessment) identified?

### Cost Estimate

At CD-0, a ROM (rough order of magnitude) estimate with a wide range is expected.
A Class 5 estimate per AACE RP 17R-97 is appropriate.

**Charge Questions:**
1. Is a ROM estimate provided that is consistent with the scope of the mission need?
2. Is the estimate range appropriately wide (e.g., −50% to +100%) given the lack of scope definition?
3. Has the estimating team acknowledged the basis and limitations of the ROM estimate?

## CD-1 / Pre-FEED: Approve Alternative Selection and Cost Range

### Risk Register

The risk register should be more developed, covering the selected alternative's technical,
schedule, regulatory, and procurement risk profile. Expect 10–20 risks.

**Charge Questions:**
1. Is the preferred alternative selection justified by an analysis of alternatives (AoA)
   that addresses cost, schedule, technical, and risk factors?
2. Is the initial risk assessment of sufficient quality to support the selected alternative
   and the cost range at this phase?
3. Are major technical risks associated with the selected alternative identified,
   particularly technology readiness risks?
4. Is the cost range adequately risk-adjusted for the level of scope definition at this phase?

### Schedule

At CD-1, the schedule should reflect the preferred alternative and provide a credible
path to the Performance Baseline (CD-2).

**Charge Questions:**
1. Does the schedule reflect the selected alternative and show a credible, logic-driven path
   to the Performance Baseline gate?
2. Are long-lead procurement items (major equipment, specialty materials) identified in the
   schedule with realistic lead time assumptions?
3. Are key external dependencies (permitting, utility connections, regulatory approvals)
   identified and reflected in the schedule logic?
4. Is the schedule consistent with the cost estimate phase durations and escalation assumptions?

### Cost Estimate

At CD-1, a Class 4 estimate per AACE RP 17R-97 is generally expected. Parametric and
factored methods are acceptable; the basis of estimate should be documented.

**Charge Questions:**
1. Is the estimate at the appropriate level of maturity for the CD-1 phase (Class 4 or equivalent)?
2. Is the basis of estimate (methodology, data sources, escalation basis, exclusions)
   documented in sufficient detail?
3. Is the contingency allowance consistent with the level of scope uncertainty at this phase,
   and is the method for determining contingency stated?
4. Is the cost range consistent with the level of scope definition, and does it bound the
   reasonable range of outcomes at this phase?

## CD-2 / FEED Complete: Approve Performance Baseline ← Primary Focus

This is the most consequential gate. Work products must be mature — complete, current,
defensible, and integrated with each other. Vagueness is not acceptable at CD-2.

### Risk Register

**Charge Questions:**
1. Is the risk register complete — does it identify all material risks to the project's
   scope, cost, schedule, and execution strategy at this phase of development?
2. Are risk statements well-formed, specific, and traceable to project data (not generic
   or tautological)?
3. Is the scoring methodology documented, consistently applied, and defensible?
4. Are mitigation strategies substantive — do they reduce probability or impact, or are
   they surveillance actions only?
5. Is each risk owned by a named individual with authority to execute the mitigation?
6. Have residual risks been assessed after mitigation, and are residual scores defensible
   given the mitigation actions proposed?
7. Are all material risks (impact ≥ Medium) integrated into the Monte Carlo cost and
   schedule risk model?
8. Are correlation assumptions between related risks documented, and does the Monte Carlo
   model reflect realistic correlation between supply chain, labor, and commodity risks?
9. Is the P80 cost and schedule outcome reasonable for a project of this type and phase —
   not so narrow as to suggest the model is understating risk?
10. Are there identifiable risk categories that are conspicuously absent from the register
    given the project type, site conditions, and execution strategy?
11. Does a sensitivity analysis (tornado chart or equivalent) identify the dominant risk
    drivers, and do the dominant drivers align with the project's known challenges?
12. Is the risk register current — updated within 90 days and reflecting the current state
    of procurement, design, and site conditions?

### Schedule (IMS)

**Charge Questions:**
1. Is the IMS resource-loaded, logic-driven, and free of open-ended activities (no
   activities without both predecessors and successors, except project start and finish)?
2. Does the critical path reflect the true project-driving constraint, and does it make
   logical sense given the project scope and execution strategy?
3. Is the schedule free of excessive constraints and hard dates that override logic —
   do imposed dates reflect genuine external constraints or internal optimism?
4. Have long-lead procurement items been integrated into the IMS with realistic delivery
   durations, and are purchase order milestones reflected in the logic?
5. Is the total float on the critical path plausible for the project type and phase —
   zero float is not automatically correct; negative float is always a red flag?
6. Has a schedule risk analysis (SRA) been performed, and does the P80 schedule outcome
   provide adequate basis for the proposed baseline completion date?
7. Are the schedule durations consistent with the resource plan — can the productivity
   assumptions be supported given the planned crew sizes and work hours?
8. Does the IMS reflect all dependencies between major contracts, vendors, and systems
   that could create interface-driven delays?

### Cost Estimate

**Charge Questions:**
1. Is the estimate at the appropriate level of maturity for the CD-2 phase (Class 2/3 per
   AACE RP 17R-97), with sufficient quantity takeoffs and unit costs to support a
   Performance Baseline?
2. Is the basis of estimate documented in sufficient detail that an independent estimator
   could reconstruct the major cost elements from the BOE narrative?
3. Are escalation and commodity price assumptions current, documented, and consistent with
   the proposed schedule for expenditure?
4. Is the contingency allowance derived from the quantitative risk analysis, and does it
   reflect the P80 minus the deterministic estimate? Or, if not QRA-based, is the method
   clearly stated and defensible?
5. Are the cost estimate and the IMS integrated — do the planned expenditure curves align
   with the schedule, and do time-related cost risks (extended overhead, escalation) appear
   in both models?
6. Is the Management Reserve (if applicable) sized appropriately relative to the known
   unknowns at this phase, and is it treated separately from contingency?
7. Are scope exclusions explicitly listed, and are they consistent with the project scope
   basis document and the IMS?

## CD-3 / Construction Start: Approve Construction / Execution

### Risk Register

The risk register should reflect updated conditions — pre-bid risk has been replaced
by contract-award risk, procurement risk has evolved, and execution risk is now primary.

**Charge Questions:**
1. Has the risk register been updated since CD-2 to reflect risks resolved through
   procurement, design completion, and permitting, and new risks that have emerged?
2. Are execution-phase risks (construction sequencing, labor, subcontractor performance,
   weather, commissioning) adequately identified and scored?
3. Are mitigation strategies owned by the execution-phase team (not the design team)?
4. Does the contingency reserve remaining in the baseline reflect the current risk profile
   after CD-2 risk burn-down?
5. Are risks associated with the current market environment (commodity prices, labor
   availability, supply chain lead times) current and not based on pre-construction data?

### Schedule (IMS)

**Charge Questions:**
1. Is the construction IMS finalized and accepted by the general contractor and major
   subcontractors as a basis for progress measurement?
2. Are all subcontractor schedules integrated into the IMS, or is the subcontractor
   scope a black box in the master schedule?
3. Are commissioning and startup sequences reflected in the IMS with realistic durations
   and system turnover logic?
4. Is the EVMS (Earned Value Management System) live and the IMS structured as a valid
   performance measurement baseline (PMB)?
5. Does the IMS reflect any contract changes from CD-2, and have the float impacts of
   those changes been assessed?

### Cost Estimate

**Charge Questions:**
1. Is the cost-to-complete estimate current and reflective of actual contract awards,
   not pre-bid assumptions?
2. Has the contingency been re-baselined since CD-2 to reflect risk burn-down from
   completed procurement and design milestones?
3. Is the cash flow forecast consistent with the contractor payment schedules and the IMS?
4. Are there any scope gaps between what was bid and what is required to achieve the
   project's performance parameters?

## CD-4 / Project Completion

### Risk Register

**Charge Questions:**
1. Have all open risk items been resolved, transferred, or formally accepted?
2. Are residual risks to operations (latent defects, warranty claims, post-commissioning
   performance risks) identified and handed off to the owner's operations team?
3. Does the final risk register record provide a useful historical baseline for future
   similar projects?

### Schedule

**Charge Questions:**
1. Has the IMS been updated to as-built status, and does it provide an accurate historical
   record of the project execution?
2. Have all schedule variances been explained in terms of approved changes, owner-directed
   changes, or contractor performance?
3. Are all contract milestones either achieved or formally resolved through change orders
   or documented settlements?

### Cost Estimate / Final Cost Report

**Charge Questions:**
1. Has a final cost report been produced that reconciles the approved Performance Baseline
   to actual costs incurred, with variances explained?
2. Have all contractor final invoices, change order settlements, and retainage releases
   been processed?
3. Are lessons learned documented in a format that can inform the cost estimating basis
   for future similar projects?

## General Charge Questions (applicable at any phase and work product)

**Currency and Document Control:**
- Is the work product current (updated within 90 days of the review, or more recently
  if the project is in a high-change period)?
- Do document headers, revision dates, and version numbers indicate this is the
  document of record — not a superseded version?

**Integration Across Work Products:**
- Are the risk register, IMS, and cost estimate internally consistent — do risk impacts,
  schedule durations, and cost contingency tell the same story?
- Have findings and recommendations from the prior review cycle been addressed, and is
  there documented evidence of closure (not just acknowledgment)?

**Team Capability:**
- Is the project team's process for maintaining this work product (risk management,
  scheduling, or estimating) mature enough to keep it current through the next phase?
